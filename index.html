DfE Design

The Existing DfE Model

  • Supplier-funded install. Solar is installed, financed and maintained by a PPA provider
  • Zero capital from HMG or schools; stays off public balance sheets
  • School buys the power at a fixed (+CPI) tariff for 20–25 years
  • Delivered via RDPs. Regional Delivery Partners batch schools, procure finance and run feasibility
  • DfE benchmarks pricing to protect value for money
  • Pilot then scale. 150 schools in RRP Pathfinder 1, with national rollout if VfM is proven
Scope of Work

  • Developed delivery options (Primary Focus): funding and delivery models sized for the real estate, with risks mapped out
  • Market health (Flagged for further work): we touched on battery and tariff costs and grid capacity, but this area needs more depth

Value Left on the Table

Where the Base Model Falls Short

  • Export income goes to the supplier. Surplus solar sold to grid; income booked to supplier. A re-negotiated share returns it to schools.
  • Export priced at 7p flat for 25 years. A realistic ~10p (Ofgem, 2025) lifts the value of every exported unit.
  • Yield set low (~801 kWh/kWp). This sits well below the UK average of ~960, and the ~1,000+ typical of southern England. Real output is probably higher.
  • Empty-summer surplus. Schools are closed for roughly 175 days a year, including the six-week summer peak. More solar is exported than the 75% self-consumption assumption implies.

Self-consumption overstated (75% vs ~50%)
Schools closed ~175 days/yr including summer peak; base assumption overstates on-site use and understates export.
High
Model on real half-hourly metered data; capture export value for schools via renegotiated share
03
Building age, condition and roof suitability
Aging roofs and RAAC-affected buildings may not support solar installation; structural survey essential before commitment.
High
Structural and roof survey first; skip RAAC / poor-condition blocks (NAO, 2023)
04
Grid export constrained (DNO / G99)
Distribution network operator limits on export capacity can reduce the value of exported generation.
Medium
Use storage and grid headroom as a site-selection criterion from feasibility stage
05
Falling rolls: sites may shrink or close
Demographic decline could mean schools close or shrink mid-contract, leaving PPA obligations without an occupier.
Medium
Cluster to absorb single closures; build sensible exit terms into PPA (NAO, 2026)
06
Inflation on the 25-year tariff
Uncapped CPI escalation over 25 years could erode the savings proposition if inflation runs persistently high.
Medium
Cap CPI escalation (e.g. 3%) paired with a floor to form a collar protecting both parties
07
Underperformance / supplier risk
Panels underperform or supplier fails to maintain, reducing generation and value without recourse.
Medium
Build in a performance guarantee so the supplier compensates any shortfall and is contractually obliged to maintain the system

Summary
Design the value back to schools

Three principles, zero extra cost to schools or HMG.

1
Value
Take a school from roughly £6.4k to £11.6k per year in energy savings, with zero school capital. That gap is a design choice, not a budget line.
2
Fit
Size the solution to the real estate. Cluster small primaries first, retrofit existing arrays and add battery storage only where the flexibility revenue actually justifies it.
3
Fairness
Use gain-share and pro-rata distribution with a guaranteed floor for the smallest schools. Every school should benefit from this programme, not just the large urban ones.
Additional Findings
Supporting evidence from the wider research

Three independent findings that corroborate the core analysis.

🗺️
Fairness and RDP Pipeline Tool
A pipeline management tool for Regional Delivery Partners can help ensure solar opportunities are distributed fairly across school types, sizes and regions. Without it, the easier large urban sites will absorb most of the early benefits.
🏛️
Portsmouth City Council: A Live Precedent
A clustered, public-sector solar PPA is already operational at Portsmouth City Council. This demonstrates the model is commercially viable at scale in a UK public sector context, not just theoretical.
⚡
Great British Energy: Independent Corroboration
The GBE schools solar programme independently mirrors our recommendations: the site keeps the export, clusters are targeted by deprivation, and DESNZ acknowledges savings are price-sensitive. This corroborates our value-capture, equity and NPV conclusions (GB Energy, 2026).
